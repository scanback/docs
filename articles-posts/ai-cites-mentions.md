# Should AI Be Cited? No. Should AI Be Mentioned? Sometimes.

When writing in collaboration with AI, I recommend these simple practices.  

1. Never cite AI or refer to it as a source. Everything AI does is a derivative of other sources, so AI cannot be a source. You may ask it what the underlying source is, and then cite this.  

2. Mention how AI was used if it is **material**. If humans had participated in the effort so extensively that they would be mentioned, then AI participation should be mentioned if it participated at this lebel. HAi deserves ad much mention as humans, neither more orless.  For example:  
    - *AI was used for extensive brainstorming.*  
    - *AI was used to generate the entire document.*  
    - *AI was used to generate all text.*  


In the future, a simplied system may emerge such as:  
⓪ = No AI &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ① = Slight AI &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ② = Moderate AI &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ③ = Deep AI

The same applies to other uses of AI such as coding and quantitative analysis.

This is the honest aproach in 2026. In a few years these recommendations will look quaint because then it will be assumed that everything except perhaps the creative process is AI.
